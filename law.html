Different law Streams.

1.Constitutional Law

The Constitution is deemed to be the supreme law of the country. It governs the affairs of the country and involves rights and duties of the government as well as its citizens. The study of the Constitution is one of the most important subjects in law school. The practice of constitutional law is one of the most lucrative areas of the law in India. Most top lawyers in India will be constitutional experts due to years of practice and experience. Similar to the practice of human rights law, the constitution is a vast area of the law and can have many cases and jurisprudence to keep track of. These cases require you to access the High Courts and Supreme Court as some of these issues can be of great significance to the justice system. Most landmark judgments in India will have a constitutional law aspect built into them somewhere.

2.Corporate Law

The field of corporate law deals with rules pertaining to the facilitation and management of business. A corporate lawyer can advise on an agreement, write up a contract, or negotiate clauses on behalf of a client. Corporate lawyers do not go to court, but rather advise clients from the comfort of their office. An understanding of the Companies Act, 2013, and the Indian Contract Act, 1872, is a must. This is one of the most lucrative areas of practice in recent years, with young graduates flocking to prestigious law firms to practice corporate law because of the attractive pay packages. Life as a corporate lawyer will involve very long hours. However, a strong understanding of corporate law can ensure you can work for many different types of clients such as companies, start-ups, banks, etc.

3.Criminal Law

This is an area of the law that is often considered the most interesting and involves defending criminals or advocating for the innocent in cases of murder, rape, theft, etc. Criminal law requires an understanding of court procedures, police investigations, and the Indian Penal Code, 1860. Typically, lawyers who enjoy court - facing lawyers practice criminal law. Most law aspirants dream of practicing in the courtroom as criminal lawyers due to the field being intriguing and interesting. One will see that there is a tendency to avoid practicing criminal law in India due to safety issues and low pay, as compared to salaries in law firms at the start of your career. However, the number of criminal law cases is on the rise and practice in the field pays well as you move ahead in your career if you are first generation lawyer. The most successful and well-known lawyers in India are criminal lawyers.

4.Human Rights Law

Human rights lawyers advocate on behalf of victims who have had their rights infringed. In India, the practice of human rights law can be long and arduous as one tries to get their day in court – however, this can also be one of the more satisfying areas of the law as often there is a vulnerable party at one end who needs legal advice, as well as the relief. To be a good human rights lawyer, one needs to be aware of basic rights that are enshrined in the Indian Constitution. . One may also see human rights principles as reflected in procedural laws in India, such as the Code of Criminal Procedure.
